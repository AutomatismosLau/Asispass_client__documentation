## 5. Privacy and Data Protection

### 5.1 Regulatory Compliance

Personal data processing is carried out in accordance with:

- **Law No. 19,628** on the Protection of Private Life (Chile)
- **Regulation of Law No. 19,628**, approved by Supreme Decree No. 355 of 2005
- Applicable Chilean labor legislation

### 5.2 Data Collected

- **Employee personal data:** name, RUT (tax ID), contact information, biometric data (fingerprints/facial recognition).
- **Attendance data:** clock-in and clock-out records, schedules, locations (for remote clock-ins).
- **Licensee data:** company name, RUT, address, contact information of representatives.

### 5.3 Security Measures

- 🔒 Data encryption in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest
- 🔐 Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
- 👤 Role-based access control (RBAC)
- 📋 Periodic security audits
- 💾 Backups and disaster recovery plans
- ☁️ Compliance with AWS security standards

### 5.4 Data Deletion

- When an employee leaves, their **contact information and identifying personal data** are deleted from the system.
- **Attendance records** are retained for **5 years** in accordance with Chilean labor law.
- Personal data is **never exported** outside the territory of Chile.

### 5.5 Data Ownership

Attendance and clock-in data are the property of the **Licensee** and the **Dirección del Trabajo de Chile**. AutomatismosLau LTDA. acts solely as custodian and data processor.
